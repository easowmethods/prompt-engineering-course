📖 Best Practices

Quick reference guide from the Google Whitepaper.

1. The 4 Pillars of Anatomy

Every prompt should ideally have: [Persona] (Act as an expert), [Task] (Summarize this text), [Context] (Here is the text), and [Format] (Output in 3 bullet points).

2. Be Specific & Direct

Leave no room for interpretation. Clearly define the task, format, and tone. Positive instructions ("Do X") work better than negative constraints ("Don't do Y").

3. Use Delimiters

Separate your instructions from the data. Use XML tags like <context>, quotes """, or markdown ### to prevent prompt injections and confusion.

4. Force Reasoning (CoT)

For math or logic, append "Let's think step by step" or provide an example where the model shows its work before answering.

5. Step-Back for Abstraction

If a task requires deep domain knowledge, ask the model to retrieve the general principles or formulas first, then ask the specific question in a second prompt.
